Why Choose PDR Over Traditional Body Work?

Paintless dent repair is the preferred method for removing dents when your paint is intact. It's the same technique used by dealerships and insurance companies because it preserves your vehicle's value.

Traditional body shop repairs require sanding, body filler, primer, paint, and clear coat - which can never match your factory finish perfectly. PDR maintains your original paint, keeping your car's history clean and value high.

⚠️ Act Fast on Dents!

Arizona's extreme heat can make dents harder to repair over time. The metal "remembers" the dent shape when repeatedly heated to 120°F+ and cooled. Fresh dents are always easier and cheaper to fix. Plus, any paint cracks from the impact can quickly lead to rust during monsoon season.

Paintless Dent Repair FAQ

How long does PDR take?

Single door dings: 15-30 minutes. Multiple dents: 1-3 hours. Hail damage: 1-2 days depending on severity. Most PDR is same-day service.

Will PDR work on my dent?

If the paint isn't cracked or chipped, PDR can likely fix it. Text us a photo for free assessment. About 80% of minor dents are PDR candidates.

Is PDR permanent?

Yes! Once the metal is restored to its original position, it stays fixed. We offer warranties on all PDR work.

Does insurance cover PDR?

Yes, most comprehensive coverage includes PDR for hail damage. For door dings, it depends on your deductible. We work with all insurance companies.

Can you PDR aluminum panels?

Yes, but aluminum (like on F-150s) requires special techniques. We're trained in aluminum PDR but it may take slightly longer.

What if PDR doesn't work?

We'll tell you upfront if PDR won't work. If we attempt repair and can't achieve perfect results, we only charge for the improvement made.

Can you remove dents from motorcycles?

Yes! We PDR motorcycle tanks, fenders, and fairings. The process is similar but requires specialized tools for smaller panels.
